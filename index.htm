February
Scott Sampson

What’s 75 million years old and brand spanking new? A teenage Utahceratops! Come to the Saroyan, armed with your best dinosaur roar, when Scott Sampson, Research Curator at the Utah Museum of Natural History, steps to the podium.

Scott Sampson is a Canadian-born paleontologist who received his Ph.D. in zoology from the University of Toronto. His doctoral work focused on two new species of ceratopsids from the Late Cretaceous of Montana.

Following graduation in 1993, Sampson spent a year working at the American Museum of Natural History, then five years as assistant professor of anatomy on Long Island. He later moved to the University of Utah.

He has conducted paleontological work in Zimbabwe, South Africa, Madagascar, the U.S., and Canada. He also hosted the Discovery Channel’s Dinosaur Planet and wrote Dinosaur Odyssey.
